Barbie History

Barbara Millicent Roberts is a fashion doll released in March 1959 by the American toy company Mattel, Inc. She has undergone numerous transformations since her debut in 1959, and she now comes in a variety of styles.

Ruth Handler wished for a more grown-up doll for children to play with after seeing her daughter Barbara assign adult roles to her dolls, as most toy dolls during the 1950s were modeled after infants. On a trip to Germany with her husband, a co-founder of mattel, she discovered Bild Lilli Dolls, which inspired Barbie dolls.

  • Around 1959, the first barbie collection was released which was composed of seven variations of blonde or brunette dolls wearing a striped swimsuit and accessories. From then on 'til the present, they have consistently released different barbie dolls and evolved along with the fashion trends of society.
  • By 2001, Barbie started to release movies starting with Barbie in the Nutcracker, a computer-animated musical fantasy film which brought magic to its watchers. A movie that truly brought something never before seen in the mainstream media.
Barbie Movies

Barbie Movies are all CGI/computer-animated feature films and streaming television films that are central to Mattel's multimedia franchise of Barbie, a fashion doll manufactured by the American toy and entertainment company.

From 2002 to 2017, these films aired on Nickelodeon in the United States. They were also released on home video formats until 2017, primarily by Universal Pictures Home Entertainment. Mattel expanded the franchise beyond films to other audiovisual media such as web series, television shows, and streaming television content from 2012 to the present, with the latter following a growing rise and trend in streaming services and online platforms.

In 2020, Mattel revamped the films into streaming television films, brand them as animated "specials" and revolve them around the canon of the streaming television series, Barbie: Dreamhouse Adventures. Additionally, a live-action adaptation of the toyline is currently awaiting its American theatrical release on 21 July 2023 by Warner Bros. following production wrap-up from Mattel Films with the association of LuckyChap Entertainment and Heyday Films.
